My Poetry

COLORS

I tried to blend my pastel blue and lilac soul with your heart, but you were too saturated.

You could not compliment my vibrant and intense strokes.

Instead, you left splatters of monochromatic colors on the walls.

They still linger in my mind. Oh, how I wished I could repaint us.

RAGE

I want to bleed into you all of the things I have known, that way you could understand the war that plagues my mind.

For, I really need to make sure I engrave your heart with all the things that I have seen, all that I have felt.

And most of all entrust you with the wirings of my mind.

GROWING

It's 3AM, the autumn air smells of old books and candle fire.

For, I am content.

Like city lights stationed in dark streets, I am growing.

INSAINITY

I wanted someone to touch upon this insanity to understand it, to admire it, to not fear it, to see this insanity for what it really was.

I have not lost my mind, I have finally found it.

If only you had saw that, instead you misinterpreted my passions and compared them to normalcy.

And well, that was where you fell short.

My passions could never be normal.

They were wickedly extraordinary.

BURN

I ache, I burn, and feel everything so very deeply.

The books I read, the music I listen to, the coffee shops I visit, the people I talk to, I give and give, not asking for anything in return.

But they too give to me.

In secret I turn them into poetry.

CRITICS

Her life is a flickering flare surrounded by a dark abyss, each spark of happiness dulls in intensity.

Light repels her.

How can she escape the thoughts that plague her mind?

It entices her, summons her being, it ignites her soul, it lights up her essence.Its what makes her tick.

Now, she is in ruins, but she does not mind it.

Not realizing the consequences, she slowly begins to perish.

She is infused with madness forver, her thoughts will live forver, with her-in her. She is her own worst critic.
