About Me

My name is Maliya. Currently, I am studying political science and creative coding. Political activism and writing is something I hope I can connect with my computer science interests in the future.

My inspiration stems from Angela Davis and other women leaders like Benazir Bhutto and Tarja Halonen. These women encourage and motivate me everyday that we still have a lot to achieve in terms of equality in society. When I read about all of their accomplishments and what they have done for human rights, I think to myself that I want to make a similar change after higher education someday. Growing up poor in the U.S and struggling with my culture did not mean I was bound by the same struggles women in my home of Kashmir were. It made me realize that I must be the voice of those who are silenced and have no way to speak for themselves.

Today, many men and women feel a womans place is in the home. This is just one of the many value assumptions that our society places on women. For many years, I assumed my house held liberal ideals. Coming from a single parent home, I thought my mother, who raised me to be a strong individual, was open-minded. However, I was wrong. As I grew older, my views and values, although somewhat similar to those of my mothers, somehow turned out to be different. Ever since I can remember, my mother has always worked and provided a decent home for me and my sister. She always taught us to be independent and to never depend on anyone, so you can imagine I was surprised when she told me that it is a womans duty to take care of her man. My mother believes that a woman is supposed to serve her husband and the husband controls everything in the home. She also feels that a woman is not lady-like if she does not wear a dress. What exactly is lady-like? Why must a woman carry such responsibility? Questions like these triggered my search for my own identity as a woman and who I wanted to be. I began to think about my own standards and what I truly wanted from life.

This is the reason I want my poetry and work to resonate with other women of color who are told they should not pursue their dreams.
